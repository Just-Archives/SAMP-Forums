There can be several reasons for this.

If they are moving, you need to lead your target (shoot in front of them - where they are running towards). This is due to lag. Where you see them is where they were a fraction of a second ago. If you shoot where YOU see them, they will see you shoot BEHIND them.

If they have god mode on (either through a server script or via cheats/hacks) you can't damage them.

If they are paused, taking a screenshot, desynced (or you are desynced) or about to crash they will not take damage.


This is not a bug with the SA-MP software and should not be in this section.

Thanks for the help.
Blood spray is a client side event.

Players taking damage is server side.

Please understand that for a server side event to take place and be the cause of something done on the client side, there will be latency or lag as its more commonly known.

Here is a small diagram of what is happening:

- Player1 shoots Player2
Client Side: Blood Squirts out of Player 2 (this is a client side reaction!!), Player 1's client sends shot (packet) to server
Packet travel:
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - (~150ms later)
Reaches server! OK
Server Side: Packet reaches server. Server checks if player is in that position...
...
Player is not in that position due to time of packet arrival!! Player is not injured.

I'm going to close this thread before it turns into a flame war.

As MP2 and Schurman explained, the issues you are describing are not bugs. The best way to have maximum impact on a player during a deathmatch is to compensate for lag using a technique called 'lead aim'.

You have to remember that GTA:SA is not an open source piece of software and a lot of hacking and cracking is required to turn it into a multiplayer game. Getting such a difficult element such as shot accuracy perfectly working is not an easy job, especially when it is not designed for this type of gameplay.
